Dining Habits of Japanese Prime Minister Fumio Kishida

An Analysis of Activities Since October 2021

By Riyo Nakasaka

Since assuming office in October 2021, Japanese Prime Minister Fumio Kishida has frequently hosted lunch and dinner meetings to engage with politicians and private individuals. Data compiled by the Nikkei newspaper from the "Prime Minister’s Official Activities" indicates that Mr. Kishida has participated in 209 dining engagements to date.

Among these, Japanese cuisine—particularly seafood, sushi, sukiyaki, and shabu-shabu—has been the most commonly chosen, with 115 instances. Chinese cuisine follows as the second most frequent choice, with 27 visits. Additionally, Mr. Kishida has dined at steakhouses 20 times and French restaurants 17 times. On some occasions, meetings have taken place in hotel rooms rather than restaurants, making it challenging to determine the specific types of food consumed during these interactions.

A detailed chart categorizes Japanese cuisine into various genres and outlines the types and names of restaurants frequented by the Prime Minister. This analysis reveals that seafood is the predominant choice among Japanese foods. Furthermore, there is a notable preference for establishments specializing in beef dishes, such as sukiyaki and steakhouses, indicating a strong inclination towards beef in Mr. Kishida’s dining preferences.

Prime Minister’s Most Frequently Visited Restaurants

YAMAZATO

The most frequently visited restaurant by the Prime Minister, Yamazato, is situated on the 4th floor of the Hotel Okura Tokyo in Minato-ku, Tokyo, with dining costs ranging from approximately $207 to $280 per person.

SENBADURU

The second most frequented venue, Senbaduru, is located on the lobby level of the Hotel New Otani in Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo, with a dining budget between $110 and $210 per person.

Notably, both restaurants are within a 2 km radius of the Official Residence/Prime Minister's Residence. Additionally, with the exception of dining during business trips, all other engagements are held within a 5 km radius of the Official Residence/Prime Minister's Residence. This proximity underscores the Prime Minister's preference for local dining options close to his official residence.
